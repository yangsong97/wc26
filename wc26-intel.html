Storylines of the Week

What the rest day is talking about
Farewell

Ronaldo's last dance ends

Tearful at 41 after Spain's stoppage-time winner, having become the first man to score at six World Cups. Neymar, too, has called time on international football after Brazil's exit.

Hosts out

The party goes on without them

All three co-hosts reached the round of 16 — and all three fell there. Canada to Morocco, Mexico to England at the Azteca, and the US 4–1 to Belgium in Seattle.

Debut darlings

Cape Verde captured hearts

Unbeaten through the group stage — including a shock result against Spain — the Blue Sharks pushed Argentina to extra time and took home the round of 32's goal-of-the-round award.

Age is a number

Messi, 39, refuses the ending

Two straight rescue acts have Argentina in a fifth quarter-final in seven World Cups. Every match now carries the same question: is this the last one? So far the answer keeps being no.
